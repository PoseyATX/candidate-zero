How to Play

A roguelike of Texas retail politics. Easy to learn; the county takes years.

The Shape of It

You are nobody, running for the Texas House. Eight weeks to the primary, six more to the general if you survive it. Before any of that counts, you must make the ballot by the end of week 8 — or the primary goes on without you.

Four Acts (same climb)

  • Act I — Primary — campaign hand, ballot doors, shop. Ballot or die.
  • Act II — General — still the same run. GOTV and contrast. Six weeks to November.
  • Act III — Session — still the same run after a general win. Legislative motions, not campaign cards.
  • Act IV — Waiting — after a loss (or rest), four interim weeks on a path kit (WA*). What you bank rides into the next filing as the same persona — you never re-pick identity.

Identity (once)

The first time you file, you draft three cards: who you are, what you run on, and where you file. Those picks lock on the Chronicle and seed your opening kit. New runs and Waiting re-files use the same identity — wipe the Chronicle only if you want a blank slate.

The Two Doors to the Ballot

Labor: petition signatures. Money: the filing fee. Most runs die at this door.

Losing Well

A run ending is not a reset. The Chronicle records every run. On a loss you choose an interim path (Act IV), take a trait, bank contacts and name, then re-file as the same persona.

Where do you work it?
